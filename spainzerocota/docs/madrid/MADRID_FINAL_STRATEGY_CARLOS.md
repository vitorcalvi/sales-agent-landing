### PHASE 2: REGISTRATION (Day 1 — The Grant Clock Starts)

**CRITICAL**: The 3-month deadline for Madrid grants starts from RETA alta date. Choose start date carefully.

#### 2.1 Modelo 036/037 (AEAT Census)
**When**: Same day as RETA alta (or day before)

**How**:
- Online: sede.agenciatributaria.gob.es → "Modelo 036/037" → "Alta en el Censo de Empresarios"
- **Key fields**:
  - **CNAE codes**: 
    - 6201: Computer programming activities
    - 6419: Financial intermediation (for day trading) — OR use 6499 for other financial services
  - **IAE epígrafe**: Choose appropriate for your activities (consult with gestor)
  - **Fecha de inicio de actividad**: Choose strategically (consider grant deadlines)
  - **Domicilio fiscal**: Madrid address (your empadronamiento address)
  - **Régimen de IVA**: General regime (Régimen General) unless you qualify for other regimes
  - **Régimen de estimación**: Usually "Estimación directa" for developers

**Cost**: FREE

**Output**: "Justificante de presentación" (save PDF immediately)

#### 2.2 RETA Alta (Seguridad Social)
**When**: Same day as Modelo 036 (or next day)

**How**:
- Online: importass.seg-social.es → "Alta en trabajo autónomo"
- **Key fields**:
  - **Fecha de inicio de actividad**: MUST match Modelo 036 date exactly
  - **Base de cotización**: Minimum €960.78/month (for Tarifa Plana)
  - **SELECT TARIFA PLANA**: Checkbox for "Tarifa Plana de 80 euros mensuales" — this is automatic for new autónomos
  - **IBAN account**: Your EU IBAN (Spanish or any EU bank) ✅
  - **CNAE**: Same as Modelo 036

**Cost**: FREE

**Output**: "Resolución de alta en RETA" (download immediately — this is your grant clock proof)

**⚠️ CRITICAL**: The "Fecha de inicio de actividad" you choose starts the 3-month countdown for Madrid grants. Don't delay.

### PHASE 3: GRANT APPLICATIONS (Within 3 Months of RETA Alta)

**Deadline**: All Madrid grant applications must be submitted within 3 months from RETA alta date.

#### 3.1 Tarifa Cero (€2,130 over 24 months)

**What it is**: 100% reimbursement of your monthly cuota (€80 + MEI tax ≈ €88/month) for 12-24 months

**How to apply**:
1. Go to: sede.comunidad.madrid
2. Search: "Tarifa Cero autónomos" or "Ayudas Tarifa Cero"
3. Select: "Solicitud de ayudas Tarifa Cero para personas trabajadoras autónomas"
4. Upload documents:
   - Resolución de alta en RETA (PDF)
   - Monthly cuota payment receipts (recibos de pago) — you'll need to pay first, then get reimbursed
   - DNI/NIE (both sides)
   - Certificado de empadronamiento Madrid (not older than 3 months)
   - Certificado de estar al corriente (AEAT + SS)
5. Submit online

**Timeline**: 
- Approval: ~30 days
- Reimbursement: Monthly, after you pay cuota
- Total: €2,130 over 24 months (€80 × 24 = €1,920 + MEI tax ≈ €210)

**Important**: You pay the €80/month to Seguridad Social first, then Madrid reimburses you. Keep all payment receipts.

#### 3.2 Fomento del Empleo Autónomo (€6,200)

**What it is**: One-time lump sum grant for startup expenses. Age 45+ qualifies for maximum €6,200.

**Eligible expenses** (must be incurred AFTER RETA alta):
- **Hardware**: Laptop, monitors, keyboard, mouse, networking equipment (router, cables)
- **Software**: Trading platforms, IDEs (VS Code, JetBrains), productivity tools (Office, Adobe), OS licenses
- **Services**: Gestoría fees (tax advisor), professional insurance (seguro de responsabilidad civil), legal fees
- **Office**: Home office furniture (desk, chair, ergonomic equipment) — only proportional portion
- **Training**: Professional courses directly related to your activity (programming, trading courses, certifications)

**Minimum expenses required**: To receive full €6,200, you need at least €4,250 in documented expenses:
- Gastos corrientes (services, software, training): €3,400
- Inmovilizado (hardware, furniture): €2,800
- Total: €6,200 (you can submit less, but grant amount is proportional)

**How to apply**:
1. Collect facturas (invoices) for ALL expenses:
   - Must have your NIE/CIF and Madrid address
   - Must break out IVA separately
   - Must have clear date (after RETA alta)
   - Must have proof of payment (bank transfer, credit card receipt, PayPal)
2. Go to: sede.comunidad.madrid
3. Search: "Ayudas fomento de empleo autónomos desempleados"
4. Select: "Solicitud de ayudas para el fomento del empleo autónomo"
5. Upload:
   - All facturas (scanned PDFs)
   - Bank payment proofs for each factura
   - Resolución de alta en RETA
   - Certificado de demandante de empleo (from SEPE, with dates clearly visible)
   - Certificado de empadronamiento Madrid
   - Certificados de estar al corriente (AEAT + SS)
   - DNI/NIE
6. Submit online

**Timeline**:
- Approval: 3-6 months (can be slow)
- Payment: Single lump sum to your bank account (EU IBAN accepted)
- **Important**: You must maintain autónomo status for 2 years after receiving the grant. If you cancel early, you must return the money.

**Strategy**: Buy all necessary equipment/services in first 3 months to maximize grant. Keep every receipt.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Registering as autónomo BEFORE becoming demandante de empleo → loses Fomento eligibility
- ❌ Using personal expenses (food, transport, non-business items) → rejected
- ❌ Invoices without IVA breakdown → rejected
- ❌ Expenses incurred BEFORE RETA alta → rejected
- ❌ Late application (after 3 months) → rejected
- ❌ Using Spanish bank account only → EU IBAN is fine, don't waste time opening Spanish account

### EU IBAN Acceptance
- Under SEPA Regulation (EU) 260/2012, Article 9, Spanish authorities MUST accept EU IBANs
- Do NOT let anyone tell you need Spanish bank account — it's illegal discrimination
- If a grant portal rejects EU IBAN, contact them citing SEPA regulation and file complaint with Banco de España
